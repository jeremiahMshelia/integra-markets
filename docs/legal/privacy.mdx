## 1. Information We Collect

We collect three distinct categories of information. They are stored
separately and never combined except where this policy expressly permits.

### 1.1 Scraped News Content

What we read so the sentiment engine can produce labels:

- News article URLs, headlines, body text, publication timestamps, and
  publisher domains
- Prediction-market event metadata from Polymarket and Kalshi public APIs
- Commodity price snapshots from Alpha Vantage
- Outputs of our own engine: classification (bullish / bearish / neutral),
  confidence scores, matched keywords, and model version tags

This category contains no information about you as a user. Where a public
article incidentally names a natural person (e.g. an executive in an
earnings report), we do not enrich or cross-reference that information.

**Source:** RSS feeds, partner APIs with commercial terms, and crawlers
that honour `robots.txt` and publisher opt-out signals. Publisher
takedown or training-exclusion requests sent to **contact@integramarkets.app**
are honoured within 30 days.

## 2. Algorithmic Data Processing — How AI Uses Data for Sentiment Models

Integra Markets is built around a sentiment engine that reads news
articles and labels them. This section explains exactly what feeds the
model.

### 2.1 What the Sentiment Engine Reads

The model's input is the **Scraped News Content** in § 1.1 — specifically
the article headline, body text (where available), publisher domain, and
commodity context.

**Your User Account Data is never used as a model input.** Your
bookmarks, votes, demographics, and preferences are not features in the
sentiment model.

### 2.2 How the Engine Produces Labels

We combine two methods:

1. A **rule-based scorer** using a finance-domain lexicon (NLTK VADER
   extended with the Henry 2008 and SentiBigNomics dictionaries) plus
   per-commodity directional rules.
2. A **small learning model** (PyTorch MLP plus a keyword bandit) that is
   trained on the engine's own prior predictions versus subsequent 24-hour
   commodity price moves from Alpha Vantage.

The current production accuracy on the Financial Phrasebank benchmark
(4,846 sentences) is approximately **69%**, comparable to inter-annotator
agreement between human financial analysts on the same dataset.

### 2.3 Optional Contribution to Model Improvement

If you cast an explicit sentiment vote on an article in the mobile app,
you may choose to allow that vote — together with the public text of the
article you voted on — to be added to our training set. We treat your
vote as ground truth for that article only. We do not train the model on
your bookmarks, saves, reading dwell time, or any other passive
behaviour. If you do not vote, no data from your account enters the
model.

**This setting defaults to OFF for all new accounts.** You may turn it on
at **Profile → Privacy → Improve the model**, and you may turn it off at
any time. Turning it off stops all future contributions immediately.
Contributions you made while the setting was on remain reflected in
already-trained model versions; we exclude your contributions from the
next full retraining cycle. Before votes enter the training pipeline,
we strip account identifiers and session metadata so that the training
set does not contain account-level information.

### 2.4 What the Model Does Not Do

- It is **not personalised** to you. The same article returns the same
  label to every caller of the same API version.
- It is **not an automated decision** that produces legal or similarly
  significant effects about any natural person. Sentiment outputs are
  informational analyses of public news. Where an enterprise API customer
  integrates our outputs into systems that make decisions affecting
  natural persons, that customer is an independent controller for that
  downstream processing and is responsible for any human-review or
  meaningful-information obligations that apply to their use.
- It does **not infer** your protected characteristics (race, religion,
  political views, health, sexuality, etc.) and does not use any such
  inference as a feature.

### 2.5 Model Versioning

Each version is tagged in the API response under the `method` field
(e.g. `commodity_vader_v2`). Major versions are retained and served for
a minimum of six months after a successor ships, so API customers can
pin a stable version.

### 3.4 Account Deletion — Step by Step

You can delete your account inside the mobile app at **Profile → Account
→ Delete account**.

1. **Day 0:** You type `DELETE` to confirm. Your account is suspended
   (no notifications, no sign-in).
2. **Day 0–30:** Cooldown. You can tap **Restore** in the in-app banner
   to cancel.
3. **Day 30:** A scheduled job deletes your `auth.users` row. Database
   cascades remove all linked rows immediately.
4. **Day 30–120:** Encrypted database backups still hold your data and
   expire on a rolling 90-day basis.
5. **Day 120:** All of your User Account Data is unrecoverable.

If you contributed sentiment votes to model training (see § 2.3), your
prior contributions remain reflected in model versions trained before
your deletion. We exclude your contributions from the next retraining
cycle. We cannot extract individual contributions from already-trained
weights; that limitation is a known property of small neural networks.

Statutory holds (billing records, fraud-prevention logs) override the
above for those specific elements only.

If you reside in California, you have additional rights under the

**Sale and sharing.** We do not sell your personal information for
monetary consideration, and we do not share it for cross-context
behavioural advertising. We do not exchange personal information for
any other valuable consideration in a manner that would constitute a
"sale" or "sharing" under the CCPA. We honour Global Privacy Control
(GPC) signals as a valid opt-out request for any "sharing" that may be
construed to occur.

**Sensitive personal information.** Account credentials and your Stripe
customer reference are "sensitive personal information" under
§ 1798.140(ae). We use these only for the business purposes permitted
by § 1798.121(a) — providing the service you requested, billing, fraud
prevention, and security — and not to infer characteristics about you.

**Right to Limit.** Because our use is limited to those permitted
business purposes, the Right to Limit Use does not change how we
handle your sensitive personal information. You may nevertheless submit
a request at **contact@integramarkets.app** and we will confirm our
use.

**"Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information"** — see
[/legal/ccpa](/legal/ccpa).

**Verification.** We may verify your identity by confirming control of
the email address on your account before fulfilling any request.

## 6. Security

We maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical
safeguards designed to protect your information:

- **Encryption in transit:** TLS 1.3 minimum, HSTS enforced
- **Encryption at rest:** AES-256 for the database, object storage, and
  backups
- **Access control:** Multi-factor authentication required for all
  administrative access; role-based access for production systems
- **Network segmentation:** Consumer Data, API Metadata, and Scraped
  News Content stored in separate database schemas with distinct
  service accounts
- **Logging:** Immutable audit log of administrative actions, retained
  24 months
- **Incident response:** In the event of a confirmed breach affecting
  your personal information, we will notify you and applicable
  regulators in accordance with the timelines required by applicable
  state law (e.g. California Civil Code § 1798.82, Massachusetts
  201 CMR 17, New York SHIELD Act).
- **Vulnerability management:** Annual third-party penetration test
  scheduled

No system is perfectly secure. We will continue to improve our security
program as Integra Markets matures past beta.
